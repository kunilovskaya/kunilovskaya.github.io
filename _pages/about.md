Since April 2026, I am with Technische Universität Nürnberg (Germany), working on natural language learning and generation in the Reconfiguration and Internalization of Social Structure ([RISS CoRE](https://gepris.dfg.de/gepris/projekt/465339976?language=en)) project.
My position is hosted by the Steffen Eger's [NLLG](https://nl2g.github.io/) research group, and I am excited to be part of this community of researchers. I have already had a chance to witness their technical prowess and strong work ethics.

This move marks my second major career shift, a long-awaited step into computational social science. For years, I explored this area through side projects, working on topics such as propaganda and social media analysis. 
I am happy to now pursue these interests as part of my core research.
It means that I am taking a break from my previous focus on translationese studies, including applications of Information Theory to interpreting, which I have been working on for the past few years ([University of Saarland, SFB1102](https://sfb1102.uni-saarland.de/)). 
I am grateful for the opportunities and collaborations that this work has brought me.
My PhD (completed March 2023, supervisor: Prof. Mitkov, UK) was on human translation quality estimation. A lot of my efforts were invested in building <a href="https://www.rus-ltc.org/static/html/about.html" target="_blank">learner parallel</a> and comparable corpora. 

Before that I held an Associate Professor position at a Translation Studies department, lecturing in Translation Studies, Theoretical Linguistics and Corpus Linguistics.
I have a PhD (Candidate of Science) in Contrastive Linguistics (completed 2004, adviser: Prof. Brodovich, Saint Petersburg University).

My research interests have shifted from corpus- and feature-based approaches to machine learning, language modelling and representation learning.
In the past few years, I was involved in several computational humanities projects, especially focused on the propaganda and social media analysis.
